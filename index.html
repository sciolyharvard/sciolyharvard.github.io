About Us

The Inaugural Harvard College Science Olympiad Invitational Tournament will be held on Sunday, January 28, 2018 on the historical campus of Harvard University in Cambridge, MA. Our Division C event will feature 30 teams competing in all 23 events. Competition events will run from approximately 7:00am to 3:00pm with the closing ceremony finishing by 6:00pm.

Registration

Registration will open on Monday, September 25th, 2017. The registration fee is $150 per team. Due to our 30-team capacity, schools may register only one team; if a second team is registered, it will be put on the waitlist. Coaches will receive a confirmation email within 24 hours of submitting the registration form.

Completion of the registration form does not guarantee participation in the tournament. Teams’ participation in the tournament is reserved only after (1) the online registration form is submitted, and (2) the registration fee is received by Harvard Science Olympiad by November 1st, 2017. Teams whose payment is not received by the deadline will forfeit their participation to a team on the waitlist. Please contact us at sciolyharvard@gmail.com with any questions

Eligibility

  • Teams must be registered with their state Science Olympiad organization prior to competing in the Harvard Science Olympiad Invitational
  • Teams must conform to the Division C membership rules found here and all competing students must conform to the eligibility rules found here
  • All students must sign (1) a liability waiver, (2) a health form, (3) a media release form, prior to competing in the Harvard Science Olympiad Invitational Tournament. Forms will be provided to coaches after registration is confirmed.

Competition Day

  • Teams must abide by the Official Code of Ethics and General Rules specified here
  • The Harvard Invitational Tournament will be scored based on the Official Scoring Guidelines specified here
  • Unless otherwise stated, the Harvard Invitational Tournament will adhere to all rules stated in the national rule book and national event clarifications posted here. In cases which the printed national rule book specifies different rules or content for different competition levels, the Harvard Invitational Tournament will follow the the rules specified for the regional tournament level
  • Each team must be accompanied to the Harvard Invitational tournament by one coach, and one chaperone (two unique adults). Every team will be given access to a homeroom in Harvard Yard where the chaperone must reside for the duration of the tournament

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can register?

Teams must be registered with their state Science Olympiad organization prior to competing in the Harvard Science Olympiad Invitational Tournament and must conform to the Division C membership rules found here. All registered Division C teams, regardless of geographic location, are eligible to attend

Why is the registration fee $150?

Our event is hosted in Harvard Yard which is the center of the oldest part of Harvard University’s campus. We understand that our registration fee is on the high end for invitationals; this is to ensure venue costs don’t inhibit us from properly executing on the other aspects of the invitational.

How will parking on campus work?

As our event takes place on a Sunday, street parking in the City of Cambridge will be free of charge. Additional parking space will be made available for volunteers pending availability.

When can I register?

Registration will open in September. For now, sign up for updates using this form (same as the green button up above) and we will let you know about any updates and remind you when the registration date gets near.

How big can my team be?

We allow teams of up to 15 high school students, no more than 7 of whom can be seniors. Every team also MUST have at least one dedicated coach AND one additional chaperone.

When will you release room assignments, event schedule, team list, etc.?

Once all teams are registered, we will be able to provide room assignments and other information. At latest, this will be three weeks before the event. We will send an email to team coaches when this information is finalized.
